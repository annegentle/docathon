Docathon

What's a docathon?

It's kind of like a hackathon, but focused on developing material and tools for documentation. Hackers of all skill-levels are welcome. The docathon will be held the week of March 6th, 2017. We'll have a kickoff event on the 6th, and then a week of hacking and documenting. We hope you'll join us in person or online!

Why a docathon?

Everybody knows how important documentation is. However, as important as it is, we often don't give documentation enough attention. Docstrings are incomplete or missing argument explanations, examples don't compile or reveal outdated APIs, tutorials are often idiosynchratic (or not even there). The docathon is about changing that.

How do I join?

We welcome both individuals and projects looking to participate, either locally or remotely. If you want to host your own remote Docathon party, click here. If you want to sign up for the event, click one of the buttons below and answer a couple questions.

The Digital Fellows, part of the Graduate Center Digital Initiatives at The Graduate Center (CUNY), will be hosting a Docathon. The first day will be focused on tutorials and getting people started. We then will have support throughout the week via our office hours and Python User's Group.

You can work on your own personal project or help out on one of the participating projects. Participants of all skill-levels are encouraged to join.
